Introduction to Photogrammetry

Course Structure: Lectures: 2 / Labs: 1 [cite: 1] | Credit Hours: 3 (2+1) [cite: 1] | Prerequisites: Nil [cite: 1]

Learning Objectives

  • To provide basic knowledge about the key elements of photogrammetry and its procedures/techniques. [cite: 1]
  • To understand stereo photogrammetry and its applications. [cite: 2]

Learning Outcomes

After studying this course, the students will be able to:

  1. Define and describe the terms photogrammetry and aerial photograph. [cite: 2]
  2. Understand the basic principles of photogrammetry. [cite: 2]
  3. Apply the knowledge to visually interpret the aerial photograph. [cite: 2]
  4. Compare photogrammetry and remote Sensing. [cite: 3]
  5. Evaluate the application of aerial photographs and its importance in natural Resource management. [cite: 3]

Course Content Overview

This course systematically progresses through the fundamental theoretical frameworks and technical applications of modern photogrammetry, organized across five core modules[cite: 13]:

  • Module 1: Introduction to Photogrammetry – Covers the core history, operational processes, end products, and the fundamental classification of remote sensing devices. [cite: 14, 15, 16, 17]
  • Module 2: Film Based Photogrammetric Cameras – Focuses on classical aerial components, camera mechanics, metrics of image motion/quality, internal orientation mechanisms, and calibration methods. [cite: 18, 19, 20, 21, 22]
  • Module 3: Digital Camera in Photogrammetry – Details modern structural digital camera geometries, dedicated calibration schemes, and practical operational environments. [cite: 23, 24, 25]
  • Module 4: Properties of Aerial Photography – Thoroughly inspects the structural classification schemas and rigorous geometric properties governing aerial imagery data. [cite: 26, 27, 28]
  • Module 5: Analytical Photogrammetry – Investigates advanced elements including interior/exterior orientations, technical stereo geometries, and mathematical orientations of explicit stereopairs. [cite: 29, 30, 31, 32, 33, 34]

Lab Outline

  • Visual interpretation, Identification and Extraction of various land features. [cite: 4]
  • DEM generation, Height measurement, scale & area calculations, and contouring by using Stereoscope and Photogrammetric Work Station. [cite: 4, 5]
